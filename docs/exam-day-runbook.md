# SET 2026 — Exam Day Runbook

**Sunday, 19 July 2026 · 21 centres · 107 schools · 9,440 students**
For **Centre Coordinators**, **Invigilators** and **KIDS Representatives**. Read it before the day.

> Everything in this document is a statement of what the system actually does. If you are asked
> something that is not covered here, say *"I will find out"* — do not guess. A confident wrong
> instruction on exam morning is worse than an honest "I don't know".

---

## 1. What the day looks like

| Time | What happens |
|---|---|
| **by 10:00** | Students report to their centre. Not their school — their **allotted centre**. |
| **10:00 – 10:30** | Students settle. They scan the QR code on their admit card and reach a waiting screen. |
| **10:30** | A **Start** button appears on each phone. The student presses it and their paper opens. |
| **10:30 – 11:00** | The online test. 50 questions, 30 minutes, on the student's own phone. |
| **11:00** | **Every paper closes automatically**, pressed or not. Phones are collected. |
| **11:30** | The offline written test begins. |

The online test **ends at 11:00 for everyone**, however late they started. A student who arrives at
10:50 gets ten minutes, not thirty. This is why reporting by 10:00 matters.

---

## 2. The three roles

### Centre Coordinator — one per centre
You are the person who holds the centre together. You do not invigilate a room.

- **Before 10:00** — Be on site by 09:00. Know which schools are allotted to your centre. Have this
  document, and the school-wise student list, on paper.
- **10:00 – 10:30** — Walk the rooms. This half hour exists so that scanning problems surface *now*
  and not at 10:30. Any student who cannot scan comes to you immediately.
- **10:30** — Announce clearly: *"Press Start now."* Then stop talking.
- **10:30 – 11:00** — Handle exceptions (§4). Do not hover over students.
- **11:00** — Announce that all papers are submitted automatically. Supervise phone collection.
- **After** — Send the KIDS office: number present, number absent, and **every exception you
  handled, with the student's 9-digit Unique ID**. Exceptions we do not hear about cannot be fixed.

### Invigilator — one per room
- Confirm every student has their **printed admit card** on the desk.
- At 10:00, ask the room: *"Has everyone reached their test page?"* Send anyone who has not to the
  coordinator **now**.
- At 10:30, tell the room to press **Start**.
- During the test: **do not touch a student's phone**, and do not answer questions about the
  questions. You may help with the *screen* — scrolling, finding a blank question, where Submit is.
- Watch for phones being used for anything other than the test. Report, do not confiscate mid-test.
- At 11:00, collect phones. Direct students to the written test at 11:30.

### KIDS Representative
- Float between rooms. You are the escalation path for the coordinator.
- Keep a written log of every incident: **Unique ID, what happened, what was done.**
- You are the one who tells the KIDS office, during the exam, if something is wrong at scale — for
  example if *many* students at one centre cannot load their paper. One student is an exception.
  Twenty students is an outage, and the office needs to know while it is happening, not afterwards.

## 3. What students have been promised

These are written on the student's own portal. They are true. You may repeat them with confidence:

- **"Your answers are saved as you go."** Every answer is stored on the phone the instant it is
  tapped, and sent to our servers about once a minute.
- **"If your signal drops, the test keeps working."** Once the paper has loaded, no signal is
  needed. The phone sends the answers when the signal returns.
- **"If your phone dies, your answers are not lost."** They charge or borrow a phone, scan **their
  own** admit card again, and come back to their paper with their answers in place.
- **"You can change any answer until you submit."** Nothing is locked in until Submit.
- **"Your paper is submitted automatically at 11:00."** Even if they never press Submit. Even if the
  phone is dead. A student who panics about this at 10:59 should be told: *it is already handled.*
- **One attempt only.** Once submitted, the paper cannot be reopened. This is deliberate and cannot
  be overridden at the centre.

## 4. When something goes wrong

| Problem | What you do |
|---|---|
| **Camera won't scan the QR** | Clean the lens. Hold the card flat, in good light, ~15 cm away. If it still fails, open **kidskolkata.org/qr** in the phone's browser — it scans the code from that page. |
| **Phone has no QR scanner at all** | Same: **kidskolkata.org/qr**. |
| **Phone dies mid-test** | Charge it, or borrow one. Scan **their own** admit card again. Their answers come back. Their deadline does **not** move — they still finish at 11:00. |
| **Signal drops mid-test** | Nothing to do. Tell them to keep answering. It is working. |
| **Two students, one phone** | Allowed, but **not at the same time**. The first must submit before the second scans. Both still finish at 11:00, so this costs the second student time — avoid it if you possibly can. |
| **"We do not hold your class"** | We have their record but no class, so we cannot give them a paper. Six students are affected. **Record the Unique ID on the screen and escalate immediately.** They cannot sit the online test until this is fixed. |
| **"Not on the register"** | The ID is not one we issued. Check the card is theirs and the number was read correctly. Escalate with the ID. |
| **"Could not be verified"** | The QR code is not a genuine one — a photo of someone else's card, or a damaged print. Escalate. |
| **Student arrives late** | Send them straight in. They still finish at 11:00. Do not give extra time — you cannot; the server decides. |
| **Student panics that they didn't press Submit** | It was submitted for them at 11:00. Say so plainly. |

---

## 5. Rules for staff

- **Never touch a student's phone.** Guide them; let them tap.
- **Never read a question aloud, or help with an answer.** Screen help only.
- **Never promise extra time.** Nobody at the centre can grant it. The clock is on our servers.
- **Never tell a student their score.** No score is shown on the day, to anybody, by design.
- **Always record the Unique ID.** Every escalation without an ID is an escalation we cannot act on.

## 6. Unresolved before the day — decisions needed

> **These are open. They are not oversights in this document; they are questions nobody has
> answered yet. Each one will become somebody's problem at 10:30 on 19 July if it is left.**

1. **Students with no smartphone.** *Undecided.* There is currently no plan and no fallback. If even
   1% of 9,440 students have no phone, that is ~94 children sitting in a room with nothing to do at
   10:30. **This needs a decision from the General Secretary before the day.**

2. **Lost, damaged or forgotten admit card.** The QR code on the printed card is the **only** way
   into the exam. There is no ID-and-date-of-birth fallback (1,005 students have no date of birth on
   file). Today, a student without their card **cannot sit the online test at all**, and nobody at
   the centre can let them in. Either we accept that, or we build a way for a coordinator to reissue
   a student's link — which needs a decision, and needs protecting, because whoever can reissue a
   link can reissue anyone's.

3. **Escalation on the day.** Coordinators' personal mobile numbers are deliberately not published
   to students. But *staff* still need a number to call when something breaks. Pending with the
   General Secretary.

4. **The four question papers do not exist yet.** IX, X, XI and XII. Nothing in this document works
   without them. This is the single largest risk to 19 July.
